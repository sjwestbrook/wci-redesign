Becky - Workforce Investment Act (WIA) Success

Becky had the perception that all college students were young. It was a perception that was proved false to her when she returned to college after losing her job as a Certified Nursing Assistant at Benedictine Manor in Aradia when it closed in 2014.

"When I thought of college, I thought of all kids, but it's not. There's a lot of older people in school."

After losing her job, she began meeting with a Workforce Connections Employment Coordinator. Her employment coordinator helped Becky apply for unemployment, assisted with revamping her resume, and the two came up with a plan for Becky's future.

It was during these meetings that Becky decided to return to college to become a renal dialysis technician. Becky was also provided with transportation and tuition assistance through the Workforce Investment Act.

Becky has two semesters remaining at Chippewa Valley Technical College in Eau Claire and has made the President's List each of her previous semesters. She says goig back to school after being out for 27 hasn't always been easy, but it's a decision she's glad she made.

"I've had a lot of support from my family, counselors at school, and my employment coordinator. I cannot say enough good things about these people."
